║           Net Monitor v3 — How It Works              ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

WIDGET
──────
A small always-on-top bar appears at the bottom-right
of your screen showing live network speed:

   ● UP: 1.2 KB/s    DN: 4.5 KB/s  ◀

● STATUS CIRCLE (left dot)
  Green  — Internet is fast (download above 1 Mbps)
  Yellow — Internet is slow (below 1 Mbps but connected)
  Red    — Internet is offline

UP / DN SPEED
  Updated every second.
  Shows B/s, KB/s, or MB/s automatically.

◀ ARROW BUTTON (right side)
  Click once  → Mini mode (just the circle ●)
  Click again → Full mode (circle + speeds)

MOVING & RESIZING
  Drag anywhere   → click and drag the bar to move it
  Resize          → drag the small grip at the bottom-right corner
  Right-click     → menu with Quit option


DISCONNECT ALERTS
─────────────────
Net Monitor checks your internet every 5 seconds by
pinging both Google (8.8.8.8) and Cloudflare (1.1.1.1).

It only alerts if BOTH servers fail TWO times in a row
(10 seconds total) — this prevents false alarms from
brief packet loss or a single slow ping.

When internet DROPS:
  - A low warning tone plays
  - A voice says "Internet disconnected"
  - A Windows notification appears

When internet COMES BACK:
  - A positive tone plays
  - A voice says "Connection restored"
  - A notification shows how long you were offline
    e.g. "Back online — was down 4 min 32 sec"


AUTO-START
──────────
Net Monitor starts automatically every time Windows
starts. No need to launch it manually.
